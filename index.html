Who?

My name is Tex and I'm from London. I started Richard Moonstreet in 2019 as I begun my journey into ceramics. I hate using my own name for new projects because they almost always go pear-shaped, and when they inevitably go wrong later, it all links back you... So Richard was my escape. My art name, or my 'Nom de Pot'. The actual name comes from a road in Crewe called Richard Moon Street (named apparently after a jolly chap who ran the London Northwestern Railway in the 1800's). Our Google Home randomly gave us directions to this street, unprovoked, at 2am one morning, so it was committed to memory. More through irritation than interest.

Even though the name was plucked out the mind of a digital assistant, I realised soon that the more I learnt about ceramic art and the more I started throwing and building my own style, the more the name started to suit what I was making. Instead of a random scrawl or stamp in the base of a vessel, which differentiated my bisqueware from other artists in the studio I worked in when it came out the kiln, it became a ceramic identity. Now I look at Richard Moonstreet and I see a unique but still familiar style, I see twists on traditional forms and shapes which are often a bit erratic. I see both bold vibrant colours of a Raku fire but also muted earthy tones of an unglazed body. But most of all I just see something fun and different. I still predominatly throw for fun and to distract from the day job, but I hope some of the things I've created spark some joy in you!

And if they spark joy, you can buy them in the online shop. Because clay is expensive!

Seasonal

I really enjoyed throwing these little trees, I love how a few embelishments to a very basic cone shape can turn the form into something so instantly recognisable.

These were hollow closed cones with ribbed bodies, a little slip on star and then a Botz green glaze with a bronze luster.

These were also the first ceramics I ever put on sale so have a place in my heart. I only threw ten and all of them vanished quite quickly into new homes.

Raku

Raku realy feels primal when you watch it. A few glazes on the surface of a piece then gas fired and carbonised in a barrel of combustables. The result when you wipe away the soot is beautiful.

There's a few Raku pieces, including this table bottle vase, in the online store!

Glazes

Studio glazes to give a real stand-out luster. This titan red glaze with undertones of blues and greys is a great colour to catch the eye.
